# Learners self-pace; Release is rolling; Criteria are revealed at Close

The Trainer opens Questions, several at a time, and Learners answer whichever open Question they choose, in whatever order. Nobody waits for the room. The Trainer still controls what is open, so a Learner never reads Question 5 while answering Question 1 unless that was intended.

Release is therefore not a per-Question act taken after the Question closes. It is a **rolling, per-Evaluation act**: proposals land on the Trainer's screen continuously, and the Trainer releases them as they come, singly or in bulk.

**Learners do not see a Criterion until its Question is Closed.** A rubric for an open-ended question is an answer key written in advance. While a Question is open, a Learner sees their Verdicts and their own Evidence — *"✓ so both sides agree where their byte counters start"*, *"✗ one thing you didn't cover"*. They know they missed something. They do not know what.

## Why Close still exists, and what it now means

Close does two things at once. It **ends answering**, cutting off anyone who had not got to that Question. And it **hands out the rubric** to everyone who did answer, which is what a Trainer does out loud when they stop taking answers and start explaining.

That second half is what makes self-pacing survivable. Without it, rolling release would put the answer key on Marie's screen at 14:03 while Jean, beside her, is still typing his answer to the same Question until 14:10.

## Considered Options

- **Trainer-paced: one Question open at a time.** Rejected. It makes the comprehension radar a clean snapshot of one Question the whole room answered together, and it makes Close harmless because everyone had finished. But it stalls fast Learners at the pace of the slowest, and a rolling worksheet is a legitimate classroom.
- **Release gated on Close.** Rejected as a consequence of self-pacing: it would hold Marie's feedback hostage to how long the Trainer gives Jean.
- **Full Criterion text released with the Verdict.** Rejected. That is the leak, in the only form that matters.
- **Criteria never shown to Learners at all.** Rejected. It closes the leak completely, but *"you missed something"* is not formative, and it undercuts the decision that made Learners a first-class audience.

## Consequences

**The Trainer's radar needs a denominator it did not need before.** When the room answered together, `criterion₂ 9/30 ✓` was a complete sentence. Now Question 1 has 27 answers and Question 3 has 4, so the read model must carry coverage — `27/30 answered` — alongside the Verdict counts. `9/11 ✓` on its own misleads.

**Close is destructive.** It ends the chance of every Learner who had not answered that Question. Under trainer-pacing this never arose. The Trainer is now making a decision about specific people, and the screen must tell them so.

**Every Evaluation has two rendering states** for the Learner: before its Question closes, and after.

**Release is per-Evaluation, and a bulk action is what keeps it viable.** "Release everything currently shown" is what preserves [ADR-0002](./0002-the-llm-proposes-the-trainer-releases.md)'s claim that reviewing is a triage rather than a re-grading. Without it, a Trainer teaching a class of thirty across five Questions performs a hundred and fifty individual approvals.
